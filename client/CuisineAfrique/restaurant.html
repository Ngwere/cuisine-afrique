Restaurant Name: 5 Fouchettes

Food Name:Ndole
Ndolé is a traditional dish of the Duala people of one of the coastal regions of Cameroon. It is a peanut-based stew made with Ndolé leaves (also called bitter leaves), crayfish, beef, and spices. The whole thing is served with miondo (a starchy mixture of ground cassava tied up in banana leaves) or plantain.

Price: 7000 FCFA
Rating: 4.5/5

Restaurant Name: Aquarius Marina 2000

Food Name:Poulet-DG
This melange of seasoned chicken and fried ripe plantain together with a flavorful tomato sauce creates a colorful gombo that tastes absolutely delicious. It is one of the best dishes to start with if you are new to Cameroonian cuisine

Price: 8500 FCFA
Rating: 5/5

Restaurant Name: Mandara

Food Name:Kondre
Kondré is a highly spiced one-pot dish that originates from the western part of Cameroon. It consists of plantain, meat (usually pork), tomatoes, onions, dry spices, and some oil.

Price: 7500 FCFA
Rating: 4.8/5

Restaurant Name: The Mouth Garden

Food Name:Yellow Soup and Achu
This beautiful yellow soup is one of the most delicate in Cameroon. It is traditionally made by fusing red oil, beef or chicken, limestone (yes, the rock!), and a variety of spices. It is paired with achu (pounded cocoyam) and sometimes a small portion of huckleberry.

Price: 8500 FCFA
Rating: 4.6/5
